What is CanSat?

Can-Sat is a project where teams design, build and supply a small satellite in a can to collect atmospheric data 🛰️. Satellites are launched in rockets up to 1 km high and recovered with parachutes 🚀. Each Can-Sat must meet specific conditions and develop an original secondary mission, to capture digital images 📸. Teams submit reports and videos to demonstrate their success, and the best will launch their satellites 🏆. The competition culminates with the selection of the winning teams, who advance to the national phase 🌍.

Fun facts

Mini but Powerful: Although the CanSat is the size of a soda can, it can carry out complex missions such as atmospheric data collection, aerial photography and even communication with other units. 🥫➡️🌐

Homemade Rockets: CanSats are generally launched from amateur rockets or model rockets that can reach impressive heights, sometimes more than a kilometer in the air. 🚀🆙

Own Parachute: After launch, the CanSat has to return to Earth safely using a parachute that the students design and test. It's a space mission style landing! 🎯

International: There are CanSat competitions worldwide, where teams of students from different countries demonstrate their skills. This promotes international cooperation and intercultural learning. 🌍🤝

Space Recycling: Many teams use recycled or low-cost materials to build their CanSats, encouraging innovation and sustainability. ♻️💡

First Steps in Aerospace Engineering: For many students, designing a CanSat is their first experience in aerospace engineering, which can inspire future careers in the space sector. 🛠️🌌
