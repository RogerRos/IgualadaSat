The satellite

One of the missions of IgdSat is to measure ionizing radiation coming from cosmic rays. 🛰️ ☢️ Cosmic radiation refers to high-energy particles ☄️, primarily protons and atomic nuclei, originating from various sources in outer space. These sources include the sun, other stars, supernovae, and even distant galaxies ☀️. Cosmic radiation can reach Earth's atmosphere and surface, posing potential health risks to astronauts and airline crews, as well as impacting sensitive electronic equipment and communication systems. ⚠️ ✅ Studying cosmic radiation is vital for space exploration , safeguarding astronaut health, understanding climate change, unraveling cosmic mysteries, and advancing astrophysics and particle physics. 🌌
